üîí Securely Sending to Your Gastroenterologist

Option 1: Patient Portal (Most Secure)

Most healthcare providers have secure patient portals where you can upload documents. This is the most secure method.

  1. Log into your doctor's patient portal
  2. Look for "Upload Documents" or "Send Message"
  3. Attach your exported CSV file
  4. Add a note: "Monthly Creon tracking data for [Month Year]"

Option 2: Encrypted Email

If your doctor accepts encrypted email:

  1. Use ProtonMail, Tutanota, or your provider's secure email
  2. Attach the CSV file
  3. Subject: "Creon Tracking - [Your Name] - [Month Year]"
  4. Body: Brief summary of any notable symptoms or patterns

Option 3: Secure File Sharing

Use HIPAA-compliant file sharing:

  • Virtru: Email encryption service
  • Secure cloud: Google Drive/Dropbox (if provider approved)
  • Set expiration dates on shared links
  • Use password protection

Option 4: Bring to Appointment

Simply bring the file on a USB drive or printed out to your next appointment.

üîí How to Securely Share Your Data with Your Doctor

Your tracking data contains sensitive health information. Follow these secure methods to share it with your healthcare provider:

Option 1: Email (Encrypted)

1. Export your data (CSV file will download)

2. Use encrypted email services:

  • ProtonMail: End-to-end encrypted email
  • Gmail Confidential Mode: Set expiration and require passcode
  • Outlook Encrypt: Built-in encryption for Office 365

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoid regular email without encryption as it contains sensitive health information

Option 2: Patient Portal

1. Export your data

2. Log into your healthcare provider's patient portal (MyChart, FollowMyHealth, etc.)

3. Upload the CSV file to "Messages" or "Documents" section

‚úÖ Most secure option - HIPAA compliant

Option 3: Secure File Sharing

1. Export your data

2. Upload to encrypted cloud storage:

  • Proton Drive: End-to-end encrypted, share with password
  • Tresorit: Healthcare-grade encryption
  • OneDrive/Google Drive: Use password-protected sharing links

3. Share the secure link and password separately with your doctor

üí° Send password via text/phone, never in the same email as the link

Option 4: Physical Copy

1. Export your data

2. Open the CSV file in Excel or Google Sheets

3. Print and bring to your appointment

üìÑ Good for in-person visits, no digital security concerns

üîí Security Best Practices

  • Never share unencrypted health data via regular email or text
  • Use unique, strong passwords for shared links
  • Set expiration dates on shared files (7-14 days)
  • Delete the file from shared locations after your doctor has received it
  • Verify your doctor's email address before sending

üí° Pro Tips

  • Export data 2-3 days before your appointment
  • Name files clearly: "John-Doe-Creon-Tracking-Jan-2026.csv"
  • Include a date range in your message to your doctor
  • Keep copies of all exports in a secure backup
  • Ask your doctor which method they prefer
